Threats to Ocean

Overfishing

Excessive fishing depletes fish populations faster than they can reproduce, disrupting marine ecosystems and food chains.

Coral Bleaching

Rising ocean temperatures cause corals to expel their symbiotic algae, leading to widespread reef death.

Plastic Pollution

Microplastics

Tiny plastic particles that contaminate marine food webs and enter the human food chain.

Ghost Nets

Abandoned fishing nets that continue trapping and killing marine life for decades.

Oil Spills & Chemical Waste

Toxic substances from industrial activities poison marine life and destroy coastal habitats.

Habitat Destruction by Sports Activities

Reckless boating, anchoring on reefs, and other recreational activities damage fragile marine ecosystems.

Climate Change & Ocean Warming

Global warming alters ocean chemistry, raises sea levels, and threatens marine biodiversity through temperature changes.

Conservation Solutions

Protected Marine Areas

Establishing marine reserves to safeguard critical habitats and biodiversity hotspots.

Rescue & Rehabilitation Centres

Facilities dedicated to saving injured marine animals and preparing them for release.

Turtle Nest Protection Programs

Initiatives to monitor and protect sea turtle nesting sites from predators and human interference.

Coral Reefs Restoration

Scientific efforts to regrow damaged coral reefs and restore marine ecosystem health.

Sustainable Fishing Awareness

Educational campaigns promoting responsible fishing practices to preserve fish populations.

Community Outreach Programs

Engaging local communities in marine conservation efforts through education and participation.
